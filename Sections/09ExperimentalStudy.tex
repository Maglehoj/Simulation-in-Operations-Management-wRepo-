\section{Experimental Study}
\label{sec:experiments}

\subsection{Scenario tree}
\label{sec:scentree}
We use a two-stage design: one-factor-at-a-time (OFAT) screening to rank the
levers, followed by a $2^k$ factorial on the highest-impact levers, then a
demand sweep for 2030 and the Industry~4.0 scrap scenario. The OFAT branch is
ordered to test the analytically predicted constraints
(Sec.~\ref{sec:capacity}) first.

\paragraph{Industry 4.0 - a throughput interaction, not just a yield gain.}
Reducing press scrap from \SI{10}{\percent} to \SI{4}{\percent} raises good
tablets per batch, but because packaging (and the constraint) time scales with
\texttt{tabletCount}, fewer scrapped tablets means \emph{more} tablets reaching
the constraint. We therefore report whether the scrap reduction translates into
higher \emph{shipped} throughput or merely shifts load onto the bottleneck
\,(\,result: [\,]\,).
