\section{Conclusions and Insights}
\label{sec:conclusions}

% ~0.75 page. Managerial takeaways, tied to the KPIs.
The baseline identifies drying as the primary constraint, at \SI{94.9}{\percent}
utilisation and with the highest required unit-hours. The cleaning team is second
at \SI{81.0}{\percent}, and packaging, although it has the largest absolute
workload, is only third at \SI{53.0}{\percent} because it runs on three parallel
lines. No capacity lever raises throughput. A second dryer relieves drying but
moves the bottleneck onto the cleaning team (\SI{81.0}{\percent} to
\SI{95.0}{\percent}), since each dryer adds a two-hour clean, and adding cleaning
staff then pushes the constraint onto packaging. Shipped output stays at
61{,}000--62{,}000 bottles in every case, because the volume is fixed by the
50-batch plan and the per-batch yield, not by station capacity. The $2^3$
factorial confirms this: no main effect is significant, and the single marginal
interaction that crosses the threshold is a multiple-comparison artefact.
Resequencing by product changes throughput by $-\SI{0.7}{\percent}$, which is not
significant, and its only benefit is a small reduction in flow time.

Towards 2030 the line absorbs demand compressed up to $\times2.0$ with zero
release violations. Congestion grows, with flow time up \SI{11}{\percent} and WIP
up \SI{12}{\percent}, but no unbounded backlog forms, and drying, not the
schedule, is the resource that would bind first if the batch volume itself grew.
The decisive lever is Industry~4.0. Predictive maintenance at the press, where
sensors catch force or fill-depth drift before it scraps tablets, cuts scrap from
\SI{10}{\percent} to \SI{4}{\percent} and raises shipped throughput by 3{,}551
bottles per run
($+\SI{5.8}{\percent}$, 95\% CI $[2{,}206,\,4{,}896]$), the only lever that
significantly raises shipped output, and it needs only a one-off sensor
retrofit with no added line capacity and no recurring cost. Our recommendation to
management is therefore to defer the capital expansion that the 2030 plan was
built around, and to invest instead in scrap reduction at the tablet press.
